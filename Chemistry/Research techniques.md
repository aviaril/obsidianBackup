# Referencing
# Research Task

### Why is referencing important
- It demonstrates wider reading and research
- supports your ideas
- enable reader to follow sources
- credits authors ideas
- avoids accusations of plagiarism
### Harvard referencing system
- in-text citation - brief way of telling a reader where information has come from
	- e.g. According to the British plastics foundation (2023), polyvinyl chloride is one of the most used polymers
	- 
#### Books
- bibliography/reference list
	- Author Surname, INITIAL(S). (Year) _Title._ Edition (if not first edition). Place of publication: Publisher.
#### Websites
Following the format:
Group or Corporate author(year site was published-last upload) *Title of webpage*.
Available at: URL(Accessed: date).

British Plastics formation(2023) Polyvinyl Chloride PVC. Available at:
https://www.bpf.co.uk/

For an in–text citation in your work, you would cite the reference as follows:

In Michael Rosen's biography (2021)...  
He began writing poetry at the age of twelve...(Rosen, 2021)

#### Web page with a group or organisation as author

##### In the text

For an in–text citation in your work, you would cite the reference as follows:

The NHS (2019) lists the main symptoms...  
The causes of diabetes...(NHS, 2019)

##### In the bibliography/reference list

**Group or Corporate author (Year site was published/last updated) _Title of web page_. Available at: URL (Accessed: date).**

NHS (2019) _Diabetes_. Available at: https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/diabetes/ (Accessed: 26 April 2021).
